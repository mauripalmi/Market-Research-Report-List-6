<p><p>Adipose Derived Stem Cell Therapy involves two main market types: autologous and allogeneic stem cells. Autologous stem cells are harvested from the patient's own adipose tissue, minimizing rejection risks and ensuring compatibility. This method is often used in regenerative medicine and aesthetic procedures. Conversely, allogeneic stem cells are sourced from donors, offering the advantage of availability and the potential for broader applications. Both types play crucial roles in advancing therapeutic strategies for various conditions, including orthopedic and reconstructive procedures.</p></p>
