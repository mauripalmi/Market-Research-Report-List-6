<p><p>Sample Rate Converter Integrated Circuits (ICs) are categorized into asynchronous and synchronous types based on their operation with audio or data signals. Asynchronous types convert signals between different clock domains, allowing for flexibility in varying sample rates without a shared clock reference, making them ideal for diverse applications. In contrast, synchronous types operate within a common clock frequency, ensuring precise timing and phase alignment, often used in high-performance applications. Both types cater to growing demands in audio, telecommunications, and consumer electronics markets.</p></p>
