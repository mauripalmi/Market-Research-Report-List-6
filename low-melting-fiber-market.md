<p><p>The Low Melting Fiber market is categorized by two main types based on melting point: fibers with a melting point of ≤130 ℃ and those with a melting point of >130 ℃. Fibers with a melting point of ≤130 ℃ are typically used in applications requiring lower processing temperatures, ideal for non-woven fabrics and thermal bonding. In contrast, fibers with a melting point of >130 ℃ are suited for applications demanding higher thermal stability, such as automotive and technical textiles.</p></p>
