<p><p>The pea protein processing equipment market encompasses two primary types: wet fractionation and dry fractionation. Wet fractionation involves using water and solvents to separate protein from starch and fiber, resulting in a higher purity protein concentrate, typically used for food applications. In contrast, dry fractionation uses mechanical processes to separate components without the use of solvents, producing a protein-rich flour. Both methods cater to the increasing demand for plant-based proteins in various industries, including food, beverages, and nutrition.</p></p>
