<p><p>Underground Gas Storage (UGS) systems are crucial for managing natural gas supply and demand fluctuations. The market primarily consists of three types: depleted fields, aquifers, and salt caverns. Depleted fields utilize exhausted oil and gas reservoirs, maximizing existing infrastructure. Aquifers involve storing gas in freshwater or brine-filled rock formations, providing flexible capacity. Salt caverns, created by solution mining, offer high deliverability and rapid response times, making them ideal for balancing peak demand and supply variations in the gas market.</p></p>
