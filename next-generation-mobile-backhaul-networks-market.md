<p><ul><li>Wired Backhaul</li><li>Wireless Backhaul</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Next-generation mobile backhaul networks encompass two primary types: wired backhaul and wireless backhaul. Wired backhaul utilizes fiber optic or copper cables to connect cell sites to the core network, offering high capacity and low latency, which is essential for data-intensive applications. In contrast, wireless backhaul employs microwave or millimeter-wave technology to link cell sites without physical cabling, providing flexibility and rapid deployment, especially in challenging terrains or urban environments. Both types aim to support increasing mobile data traffic and enhance connectivity.</p></p>
