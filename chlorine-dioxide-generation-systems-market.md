<p><p>Chlorine Dioxide Generation Systems include primarily two market types: the Acid-Chlorite Method and the Chlorine-Chlorite Method. The Acid-Chlorite Method involves reacting sodium chlorite with an acid to produce chlorine dioxide, often used for disinfection and pulping processes. The Chlorine-Chlorite Method combines chlorine with sodium chlorite in a controlled environment, yielding chlorine dioxide. Both methods cater to diverse applications in water treatment, food processing, and industrial cleaning, providing effective solutions for sanitation and oxidation processes.</p></p>
