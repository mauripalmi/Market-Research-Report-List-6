<p><p>Midline catheters are peripheral intravenous devices designed for short- to medium-term use, typically ranging from one week to four weeks. They are inserted into a peripheral vein and advanced to the middle of the upper arm, providing an alternative to standard peripheral IV lines and central venous catheters. Midline catheters offer advantages such as reduced risk of complications, ease of insertion, and increased patient comfort.</p><p>The Midline Catheters Market is experiencing robust growth, driven by factors such as the increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, the rising demand for outpatient care, and technological advancements in catheter designs. <p><p>The midline catheters market is primarily categorized into two types: silicone and polyurethane. Silicone catheters are known for their biocompatibility and flexibility, making them suitable for longer-term access and minimizing irritation. They are often preferred for their lower risk of thrombosis. In contrast, polyurethane catheters offer greater strength and are typically used for larger vessels, providing a smoother insertion experience. Both materials cater to different patient needs, influencing their applications in healthcare settings.</p></p>
