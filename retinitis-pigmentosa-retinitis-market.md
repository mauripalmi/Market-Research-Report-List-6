<p><p>Retinitis Pigmentosa (RP) is a group of inherited retinal disorders that lead to progressive degeneration of the photoreceptor cells in the retina, resulting in vision loss. Initial symptoms often include night blindness and a gradual loss of peripheral vision. As the disease progresses, individuals may experience tunnel vision and, in severe cases, complete blindness. Currently, there is no cure for RP, but ongoing research is exploring gene therapies, retinal implants, and pharmacological interventions.</p><p>The Retinitis Pigmentosa Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.5% during the forecast period, driven by increasing prevalence rates and growing awareness of genetic disorders. Technological advancements in diagnostic tools, such as genetic testing and imaging techniques, are improving early diagnosis and management. Additionally, the development of innovative treatment options, including stem cell therapy and gene editing technologies, is gaining attention, providing new avenues for therapeutic interventions. Collaborations between pharmaceutical companies and research institutions are enhancing the R&D pipeline, further fueling market growth. Increased funding for research and expanding healthcare access also contribute to the market's positive outlook, enabling better care and treatment options for patients suffering from Retinitis Pigmentosa.</p></p>
