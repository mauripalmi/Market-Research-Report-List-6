<p><p>Digital banking platforms can be categorized into two main market types: on-premises and cloud-based solutions. On-premises platforms involve installing software directly on a financial institution's servers, offering greater control and security but requiring significant upfront investment and maintenance. In contrast, cloud-based systems are hosted on external servers, providing scalability, flexibility, and lower initial costs. They enable banks to quickly adapt to changing market demands while ensuring easier updates and maintenance. These distinctions impact operational efficiency and financial resource allocation.</p></p>
